The Blending Problem

The Blending Problem involves determining the optimal mix of ingredients to create a product that meets specific property requirements while minimizing cost. This optimization problem is solved using Linear Programming techniques.

Real-World Applications

1. Manufacturing and Production

  • Metal Alloys: Mixing different metals to achieve specific strength and durability properties.

2. Food and Beverage Industry

  • Food Manufacturing: Creating recipes that meet nutritional requirements.

3. Petroleum Industry

  • Gasoline Blending: Mixing different grades of fuel to meet octane requirements.

4. Chemical Industry

  • Pharmaceutical Manufacturing: Blending active ingredients and excipients.

Mathematical Formulation

1. Sets and Parameters

  • Set of ingredients: \( I = \{1, 2, \dots, n\} \)
  • Set of properties: \( P = \{1, 2, \dots, m\} \)
  • Cost per unit of ingredient i: \( c_i \)
  • Available quantity of ingredient i: \( a_i \)
  • Property p value for ingredient i: \( p_{i,p} \)
  • Total required quantity: \( Q \)
  • Property bounds: \( [l_p, u_p] \) for each property \( p \)

2. Decision Variables

Amount of each ingredient to use:

\[ x_i = \text{quantity of ingredient } i \text{ to use} \quad \forall i \in I \]

3. Objective Function

Minimize total cost:

\[ \text{Minimize} \sum_{i \in I} c_i x_i \]

4. Constraints

Total quantity requirement:

\[ \sum_{i \in I} x_i = Q \]

Availability constraints:

\[ x_i \leq a_i \quad \forall i \in I \]

Property bounds:

\[ l_p Q \leq \sum_{i \in I} p_{i,p} x_i \leq u_p Q \quad \forall p \in P \]

Non-negativity:

\[ x_i \geq 0 \quad \forall i \in I \]

Implementation Notes

This formulation is implemented using the Gurobi optimization solver, which:

  • Creates continuous variables \(x_i\) for ingredient quantities
  • Implements the cost minimization objective
  • Ensures all quantity and property constraints are satisfied
  • Handles bounds on both individual ingredients and resulting properties
